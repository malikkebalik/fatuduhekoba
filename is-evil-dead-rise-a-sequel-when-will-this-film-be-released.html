Is Evil Dead Rise a Sequel?

In contrast to the 2013 version, Evil Dead Rise is somewhat of a continuation of the original trilogy. The new picture, which includes references to the classics, expands upon the mythos of the original films rather than being a remake. According to GamesRadar, director Lee Cronin recently discussed the film’s place in the franchise with Total Film:

“There’s no Ash in this story and no cabin in the woods; they’re two iconic elements of what Evil Dead is. But the movie does include the book and an extraordinary amount of vicious, malevolent Deadites, so I was always pretty comfortable making that move [to LA].”

The transition from the log cabin in the woods to the high-rise Los Angeles apartment complex is the one he alludes to. Although it is a radical departure from what viewers might have anticipated from a new film, the trailer has shown that it also contains much of the original’s DNA.

What is the Plot of Evil Dead Rise?

One thing has remained the same throughout the “Evil Dead” series: someone reading a lousy book, most frequently the Necronomicon, which then releases the forces of hell. In the first two “Evil Dead” films, a group of young people is picked off one by one and taken over by savage demons as this horror show plays out in a cabin in the middle of nowhere.

Of course, the main distinction between the two movies is that “Evil Dead II” is a masterful horror-comedy whereas “The Evil Dead” is a pure horror movie. In “Army of Darkness,” the franchise’s protagonist Ash Williams (Bruce Campbell) travels back to battle a skeleton army. In 2013’s “Evil Dead,” a group of friends trying to assist a lady in overcoming her drug habit decide to hide out in a cabin and unleash the Deadites, and things get even gorier.

But what will be the subject of “Evil Dead Rise”? Will it stick to the tried-and-true “Evil Dead” model of a lodge in the woods or take a more original approach like “Army of Darkness”? The Deadites are traveling to Los Angeles this time and intend to devastate an apartment building.

Who is Starring in Evil Dead Rise?

The shotgun-toting, chainsaw-wielding Ash Williams, played by the one and only Bruce Campbell, served as the franchise’s focal point in the first three “Evil Dead” movies and the Starz series “Ash vs. Evil Dead.” The 2013 film moved the focus to Mia, played by Jane Levy, a heroin addict who must contend with an attack from hell when she attempts to quit cold turkey.
