Biography:

Daphne Zuniga was born on October 28, 1962 in San Francisco, California. After high school, she attended the University of California, Los Angeles where she studied theater arts. In 1982, she made her debut in the film The Dorm That Dripped Blood. She later had her first leading role in the movie The Initiation in 1984. She later starred in the films Spaceballs, Last Rites, and The Fly II.

From 1992 to 1996, she gained further prominence courtesy of her role as Jo Reynolds in Melrose Place. She returned to starring on television with roles in American Dreams and Beautiful People. Her latest recognized role was as Victoria Davis in One Tree Hill. In the movies, she’s mostly acted in low-budget and independent productions with her most recent work in Witness Unprotected.
