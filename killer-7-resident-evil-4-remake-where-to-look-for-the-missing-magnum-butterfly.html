Killer 7 Resident Evil 4 Remake: Where to Look for the Missing Magnum Butterfly?

August 2024 · 2 minute read

Resident Evil 4 Remake features the return of the Broken Butterfly magnum. It remains the first magnum that can be purchased, making it a trustworthy early boss slayer. Due to altered stats, it belongs to a different class than the 2005 version.

Magnums are sluggish and often utilize more uncommon ammunition, despite having a lot of sheer firepowers. The Broken Butterfly is excellent against lone, powerful foes but is not always the best tool.

Gamers should carefully consider whether a shotgun, rifle or many grenades are the best items to keep in their Resident Evil 4 Remake attaché case.

The Broken Butterfly Location Is Revealed, How To Purchase?

The Broken Butterfly is currently located in the new Chapter 7 of Resident Evil 4 Remake, which still depicts the Castle region of the earlier Chapters 3-1. The vendor will begin charging 38,000 Pesetas as the normal price for the Broken Butterfly (the renamed BP currency).

However, the Resident Evil 4 Remake vendor will provide a brief 30% discount the first time you speak with him, reducing the price to 29,400 Pesetas. Those interested in a powerful but constrained weapon should purchase it before the price increases.

The numbers and performance of The Broken Butterfly have also changed. Leon’s crammed attaché case only has 3×2 places for the smaller rifle. The power, reload speed, and the Designer likewise change fire rate to higher or lesser numbers.

Also, the remake adds a new attribute called precision, which works a little like auto-aim to assist in making up for player accuracy. Each stat can be upgraded independently for even more Resident Evil 4 Remake customization.

Gamers should also be aware that during Chapter 13, the Killer 7 magnum, an even more potent but slower-firing Resident Evil 4 Remake gun, will be sold. Players should therefore focus on increasing the Broken Butterfly’s rate of fire before determining whether it is still valuable enough to become a staple of their attaché case.
