David Beckham | Career

David Beckham joined Manchester United as a trainee on July 8, 1991, and was a member of a group of young players who helped the team win the FA Youth Cup in May 1992.

He won the Premier League six times and the UEFA Champions League once with United in 1999.

After that, he spent four seasons at Real Madrid, winning the La Liga title in his final season.

Beckham signed a contract with the LA Galaxy of Major League Soccer in July 2007.

He completed two loan periods with Milan in Italy while playing for the Galaxy in 2009 and 2010. Beckham was the first British football player to play 100 games in the UEFA Champions League.

David made his international debut for England on September 1, 1996, at the age of 21.

Moreover, David served as captain for six years, gaining 58 caps.
