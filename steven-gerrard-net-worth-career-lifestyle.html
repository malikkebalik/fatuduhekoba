Steven Gerrard | Net Worth & Income

Net Worth

One of the exceptional players of his generation Steven Gerrard’s net worth as of December 2023 is a whopping $90 million.

Gerrard has earned an astonishing fortune through his successful soccer career and lucrative endorsement deals.

During his active playing career, Gerrard was one of the most sought-after players.

In the 2003-04 season, Gerrard turned down Chelsea’s £20 million transfer offer and stayed with the Reds under new coach Rafael Benítez.

Income

In 2009, Gerrard signed four years £30 million extension contract with Liverpool FC.

His previous deal with the club had paid him £120,000 a week, whereas his new deal increased his wage to £140,000 a week.

In the summer of 2015, Gerrard left Anfield.

Following that, the LA Galaxy of Major League Soccer announced the signing of Steven Gerrard on 7 January 2015.

He signed an 18-month $9 million deal with LA Galaxy as Designated Player.

Furthermore, as for his coaching, Gerrard is one of the highest earners in the league.

As the coach of Premier League Club Aston Villa, Gerrard bags around £5 million each year.

Aside from that, Gerrard makes around $4 million per year from brand sponsorships with companies such as Mars, Adidas, and Lucozade, among others.

If we are to combine his total earnings, Steven is one of the 20 highest-earning soccer athletes in the world.

As a matter of fact, Forbes listed Gerrard’s combined income from salaries, bonuses, and off-field earnings at $18.7 million in 2014.

Out of total earnings, $13.2 million came from salaries, whereas $5.5 million came from endorsement deals.

Similarly, in 2013 his total earnings racked up $17.2 million, which included $12.7 million from salaries and winnings and $4.5 million from endorsement deals.

Moreover, Gerrard was also one of the top-earning Major League Soccer players.

Steven Gerrard | Career

Steven Gerrard was born in Whiston, Merseyside, England, in 1980.

He played youth soccer from 1987 to 1998 for Liverpool.

He was later called up to Liverpool and played there for his entire career.

In 2003 he became the captain of the squad.

Similarly, Gerrard played for England’s U-21 club for one year before being called for the senior team.

He has been awarded the Ballon d’Or Bronze Award and UEFA Club Football of the year.

Moreover, Gerrard is the only player to score a goal in a UEFA Cup Final, a League Cup Final, a Champions League Finals, and FA Cup Final.
