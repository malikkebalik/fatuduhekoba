Harrisburg Melissa Deroba Accident Details

In a tragic and devastating incident that unfolded on Tuesday morning, Melissa “Missy” Deroba lost her life in a multi-vehicle accident on Rt. 283 near Roundtop Rd.

A dedicated coach at the Gymnastics Center of Hershey for 25 years, Missy was known for her unwavering passion for camping at Pennsylvania campgrounds and was an esteemed alumna of Millersville University.

The accident, around 11:02 a.m., involved two tractor-trailers and a passenger car, triggering a fire on the roadway.

Missy was driving a grey 2005 Ford Escape, rear-ended by a tractor-trailer and collided with another tractor-trailer.

Tragically, she was pronounced dead at the accident site. The initial tractor-trailer driver was taken to Hershey Medical Center for treatment.

Authorities are investigating the incident as a potential homicide due to a motor vehicle, urging anyone with footage or information about the collision to come forward, as per PSP Harrisburg’s request.

In the wake of this heart-wrenching loss, a GoFundMe campaign initiated by Mikalah Copenhaver aims to support Missy’s family with the funeral expenses.

Survived by her long-term boyfriend, John, and her daughter, Claire, as well as her cherished pets Buddy, Ginger, and Morgan, Missy’s family, unprepared for the unexpected funeral costs, has set a fundraising goal of $20,000.

Any surplus funds will be dedicated to securing Claire’s higher education, honouring Missy’s commitment to her family’s future.
